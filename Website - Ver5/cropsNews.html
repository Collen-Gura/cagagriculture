Wheat production under pivot irrigation

Green Valley Estates operates 12 pivots with irrigation capacity of 500ha. This field is under 38ha pivot. Klen Karoo wheat variety was grown in this field and a yield of 8.2t/ha is expected. Seen here is Klen Karoo inspector checking Klen Karoo variety's performance. The field had pH of 6.3 and was planted on 18 May, 2020. Organic matter for the field was 8.4%. Fertiliser, in units, was applied as follows: Nitrogen = 279kgs/ha, Phosphate = 96kg/ha, Potassium = 223kg/ha and Sulfur = 36kg/ha

Wheat Harvesting at CAG Green Valley Estate

450ha were under winter wheat at Green Valley Estate. The Estate adopted Conservation agriculture 12 years ago. Organic matter averages 8%. A yield of 7.5t/ha is expected from this field.

Soil Fertility at CAG Green Valley Estates

The Estate plans adopted Conservation Agriculture 12 years ago. No disk or plough is used in land preparation at the Estate. An 8-row strip-till acquired when CA was adopted. GPS guided tractors were acquired for use with the No-till land prep and planting. This has seen the Estate move from 1.8% organic matter to an average of 8.7% for the Estate. The benefit of No-till, or Conservation Agriculture, is that you get between 9 and 13kg per 1% of organic matter. Our fertilsier program is expected to have signficant reduction going forward, apart from major positive contribution to Climate Change. Studies have shown that 5% of organic matter leads to nitrogen release of between 45kgs and 68kgs during the growing season.

Source of irrigation water for CAG Green Valley Estates

Water adequacy is a critical factor for sustainability and viability of irrigated crop at CAG Green Valley Estates. The dam's capacity is sufficient to see the Estate go through 5-year cycle of drought, taking into account other users with similar water rights
